Swiss crust



Description


The swiss crust is a crispy, golden layer of melted Swiss cheese, often made with Gruyère or Emmental. It forms at the bottom of a fondue pot and is scraped off to be enjoyed as a savory, crunchy treat. It’s a popular snack or side dish in Switzerland, especially during fondue meals.


Ingredients for 4 persons


  • 4 slices of bread
  • 1/2 cup of dry white wine
  • 1/2 pound of "Gruyère" cheese
  • 1/2 pound of "Emmental" cheese
  • 1/2 pound of "Comté" cheese
  • 1 tablespoon of cornstarch
  • 1 tablespoon of kirsch
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 1 tablespoon of flour
  • 1 cup of milk
  • 1 pinch of nutmeg
  • 1 pinch of salt
  • 1 pinch of pepper

Steps


  1. Peel the garlic clove and cut it in half. Rub the inside of a baking dish with the cut sides of the garlic.
  2. Preheat the oven to 350°F (180°C).
  3. Grate the "Gruyère", "Emmental", and "Comté" cheeses and toss them with the cornstarch until they are well coated.
  4. Place the slices of bread in the baking dish.
  5. Heat the butter in a saucepan over medium heat until melted.
  6. Add the flour to the melted butter and cook for 1 minute, stirring constantly.
  7. Gradually whisk in the milk until the mixture is smooth and thickened.
  8. Stir in the nutmeg, salt, and pepper.
  9. Add the cheese mixture to the saucepan, a handful at a time, stirring constantly until the cheese is melted and smooth.
  10. Pour the cheese sauce over the slices of bread in the baking dish.
  11. Bake for 20 minutes, or until the cheese is melted and bubbly.
  12. Remove the baking dish from the oven and serve immediately.
